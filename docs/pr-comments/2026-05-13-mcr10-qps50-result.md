### Perf rig update — another data point at MCR=10, QPS=50, Burst=300 (noop, N=100)

Wanted to see what happens between the two earlier extremes (MCR=1 baseline and MCR=20 flat-out), so I ran one in the middle.

Flags used:

```
--dynamic-rr-max-concurrent-reconciles=10
--kube-api-qps=50
--kube-api-burst=300
--dynamic-rr-filter-noop-writes=true
```

#### Result

| run | MCR | QPS / Burst | predicate | wall-clock | p50 recon | longest_running peak |
|---|---:|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| mcr1-flagsoff | 1 | 20 / 30 | off | 2m59s | 11 | 1.12s |
| mcr1-all | 1 | 200 / 400 | on | 2m5s | 12 | 1.12s |
| mcr20-flagsoff | 20 | 20 / 30 | off | 1m40s | 14 | 3.13s |
| mcr20-all | 20 | 200 / 400 | on | 40s | 18 | 0.40s |
| **mcr10-qps50** *(new)* | **10** | **50 / 300** | **on** | **52s** | **16** | **1.29s** |

`summary.md` for this run lives at `test/perf/results/noop-mcr10-qps50/summary.md` if you want to poke around.

#### A few things I noticed

1. A halved budget seems to give most of the performance. Cutting MCR 20→10 and QPS 200→50 only slows wall-clock by ~12s vs the saturated 40s run. So at least on this workload, we don't need to push the upper end of either knob to get most of the win.
2. QPS=50 / Burst=300 looks past the throttle cliff. `longest_running` is back down to ~1.3s (roughly one state-machine step), not the 3s+ we saw at MCR=20 with the default QPS=20 — workers aren't queueing behind the rate-limiter any more.
3. Could be a reasonable middle ground? If we want a single default that's noticeably better than today without going as aggressive as MCR=20 + QPS=200/400, something around **MCR=10 + QPS=50 / Burst=300 + predicate** seems to capture most of the wall-clock improvement (~3.4× over the baseline) while staying conservative on burst budget. Open to other framings — happy to run more configurations if there's a specific point on the curve worth checking.
4. Same caveat as before: reconciles-per-RR climbs a little with parallelism (p50: 11 → 16 → 18 across MCR=1/10/20), driven by the self-watch re-enqueue interleaving we discussed. The predicate trims some of that but doesn't change the shape.
